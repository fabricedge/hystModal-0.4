Website Overview

Websites have many functions and can be used in various fashions; a website can be a personal website, a commercial website, a government website or a non-profit organization website. Websites can be the work of an individual, a business or other organization, and are typically dedicated to a particular topic or purpose.

Any website can contain a hyperlink to any other website, so the distinction between individual sites, as perceived by the user, can be blurred. Websites are written in, or converted to, HTML (Hyper Text Markup Language) and are accessed using a software interface classified as a user agent.

Monopoly Overview

Monopoly is a popular board game originally published by Parker Brothers, now a trademark of Hasbro. It is designed for two to eight players, who move around the game board, buying and trading properties, developing them with houses and hotels, and collecting rent from opponents, with the goal of bankrupting them.

The game is named after the economic concept of a monopoly, the domination of a market by a single entity. Players aim to control as much property as possible while strategically managing their money. Monopoly is widely recognized for its distinctive game pieces, colorful property cards, and iconic "Go" and "Jail" spaces.

Gameplay Mechanics

Monopoly combines luck and strategy. Players roll dice to move around the board, land on properties, and make financial decisions such as purchasing, trading, or mortgaging assets. Chance and Community Chest cards introduce unexpected events, while building houses and hotels increases rent and generates higher income.

Monopoly has evolved over the years, with numerous themed editions, digital versions, and tournaments. The game has also inspired discussions on economics, strategy, and negotiation, making it both a recreational activity and a cultural icon.
